About Ethernet

Ethernet is a widely adopted networking technology that forms the backbone of Local Area Networks (LANs) across the globe. Developed in the 1970s by Robert Metcalfe and his team at Xerox PARC, Ethernet was initially designed to facilitate communication among computers in a local network. Since its inception, Ethernet has evolved significantly, adapting to the growing demands for speed, efficiency, and reliability in data transmission.
